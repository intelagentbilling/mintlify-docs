## Overview

William Osler physicians are reimbursed by **EFT** to a bank account on file. You can add or update that account yourself — on the **web portal** or the **mobile app** — and verify it with a small test deposit.

<Note>
Self-service bank-account updating is available to physicians enrolled in the **William Osler Hospital Reimbursement** program. If you don't see these options, contact IntelAGENT support.
</Note>

### How verification works

Whenever you save **new** banking details, we send a **$0.01 test deposit** to that account (it can take up to 30 days to appear). Your reimbursements are **paused** until you see the deposit and turn on **Bank account verified**. You can turn verification off at any time — for example, if your banking information changes.

<Warning>
Real payments are only sent while **Bank account verified** is on. If you change your account, verification resets and you'll need to confirm the new test deposit before payments resume.
</Warning>

## Updating your bank account

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Desktop (web portal)">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the banking settings">
        Go to **Settings**. William Osler physicians see a **WOHS Bank Account & Reimbursement** section.

        <Frame>
          <img src="/images/docs/web-app/bank-account/01-settings-empty.png" alt="The WOHS Bank Account & Reimbursement section in Settings with an empty banking form and a Bank account verified toggle" />
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter your bank details">
        Fill in your **Institution** (3 digits), **Transit** (5 digits), and **Account number**.

        <Frame>
          <img src="/images/docs/web-app/bank-account/02-settings-filled.png" alt="The banking form filled with institution, transit, and account numbers" />
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Save your banking">
        Click **Save banking**. We send a **$0.01 test deposit** to the new account. Your saved details now show as *on file* and *not yet confirmed*.

        <Frame>
          <img src="/images/docs/web-app/bank-account/03-settings-on-file.png" alt="The banking section showing the saved account on file as not yet confirmed, with the verified toggle off" />
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify the test deposit">
        When the $0.01 deposit appears on your statement, turn on **Bank account verified**. Your reimbursements are now active.

        <Frame>
          <img src="/images/docs/web-app/bank-account/04-settings-verified.png" alt="The banking section with Bank account verified turned on and the account confirmed" />
        </Frame>
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Mobile app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open Settings">
        Tap the **Settings** tab. William Osler physicians see a **Bank account verified** toggle and an **Update banking information** row.

        <Frame>
          <img src="/images/docs/mobile-app/bank-account/01-settings.png" alt="The mobile Settings screen with a Bank account verified toggle and an Update banking information row" />
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Update banking information">
        Tap **Update banking information** and enter your **institution**, **transit**, and **account number**, then tap **Save banking**. We send a **$0.01 test deposit** to the new account, and payments pause until you confirm it.

        <Frame>
          <img src="/images/docs/mobile-app/bank-account/02-update-banking.png" alt="The mobile banking information screen with institution, transit, and account number fields and a Save banking button" />
        </Frame>
      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify the test deposit">
        When the $0.01 deposit appears on your statement, return to **Settings** and turn on **Bank account verified**. Your reimbursements are now active. You can turn it off any time if your banking details change.

        <Frame>
          <img src="/images/docs/mobile-app/bank-account/03-verified.png" alt="The mobile Settings screen with Bank account verified turned on, showing the confirmation date" />
        </Frame>
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
